Celeritas

Electrical Engineering Director (May 2010 - May 2011)

Safety Director (January 2010 - May 2010)

I joined Purdue Solar Racing in September of 2009, at the start of the two-year design and build cycle for the team’s 8th solar car, Celeritas. During this period, I was elected as Safety Director and as Electrical Engineering Director. Celeritas won first place in both the 2011 and 2012 Shell Eco-Marathons with 2,200 MPG-E and 2,325 MPG-E respectively. The team also won the Communications offtrack award, an award for the team who delivers the most impactful campaign to promote their team, for both years.

The car was named after the latin word for swiftness, celeritas. The "c" in the equation "E=mc2" is said to have come from the word celeritas, so the team decided the first three digits of the speed of light, 299, would make for a good race number. Celeritas was the team's first urban concept car, a racing category for the Shell Eco-Marathon, and the team’s first fully computerized solar car. The custom-built electronics in the car used a CAN bus for the system-level communications and PIC microcontrollers for the onboard processing.
